Privacy Policy

How VeilPay handles data

Last updated: June 23, 2026

VeilPay is built to need as little of your data as possible. There's no signup, no account, and no email required to use it. This policy explains exactly what that means in practice.

1. What we don't collect

VeilPay does not require an account. We don't collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personal identifying information to use the app. There is no login system and nothing stored about you on a VeilPay server tied to your identity.

2. What VeilPay does interact with

To use VeilPay, you connect a Solana wallet (such as Phantom). Here's what that involves:

  • Your wallet's public key is used to identify your invoices and payments. A public key is not personal information by itself — it's a public, pseudonymous address.
  • Transaction data (invoice amounts, payment status, timestamps) is used to build and submit real USDC transfers on Solana mainnet. Amounts and wallet addresses involved in a payment are visible on Solana's public ledger, the same as any standard on-chain transfer.
  • A truncated version of your public key may be stored temporarily in your browser's session storage purely to maintain your session while you use the app. This is never sent to a VeilPay server and clears when your session ends.

3. On-chain data

Solana is a public blockchain. Every payment made through VeilPay — the amount, the sender's wallet, and the recipient's wallet — is visible on the public ledger, the same as any standard on-chain transfer. VeilPay does not hide or encrypt any part of a transaction. VeilPay's Smart TX Stack (dynamic Jito tips and AI-assisted retry) exists to help your payment land reliably, not to hide it.

4. Cookies and analytics

VeilPay does not use third-party advertising trackers or sell any data to anyone, because we don't collect personal data to sell in the first place. If basic, anonymized analytics are ever added to understand traffic patterns (e.g. page views, not individual identity), this policy will be updated to reflect that before it happens.

5. Third-party services

VeilPay relies on infrastructure providers to function: Helius for Solana RPC access, and Jito for transaction submission. These providers may process technical request data (such as IP address and RPC call data) under their own privacy policies as part of standard infrastructure operation. VeilPay does not share any personal data with these providers because VeilPay itself does not collect any.

6. Your wallet, your control

VeilPay never has access to your private keys or seed phrase. Every transaction requires your explicit approval through your connected wallet. Disconnecting your wallet at any time ends VeilPay's access to your session entirely.

7. Changes to this policy

If VeilPay's data practices change — for example, if we ever introduce optional accounts or notifications — this page will be updated and the "last updated" date above will reflect it.

8. Contact

Questions about this policy can be directed to the VeilPay team via the contact details listed on our GitHub repository.
